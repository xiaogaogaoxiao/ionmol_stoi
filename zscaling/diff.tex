\begin{abstract}
In the present work, we investigate scaling rules for the ionization cross sections of multicharged ions on molecules of biological interest. The cross sections are obtained from distorted--wave calculations for atomic targets
combined with \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{a }\DIFaddend stoichiometric model for the molecules proposed in [Mendez \textit{et al.} J. Phys B (2020)]. We examine ions with charges $Z$ from $+1$ to $+8$ in \DIFdelbegin \DIFdel{the pyrimide bases: adenine}\DIFdelend \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{five nucleobases --adenine}\DIFaddend , citosine, guanine, thymine, \DIFdelbegin \DIFdel{uracil}\DIFdelend \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{uracil--}\DIFaddend , tetrahydrofuran, pyrimidine, and also in water. We propose a scaling with the ion charge\DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{, which is }\DIFaddend valid in the intermediate to high energy range, i.e. 0.2-5 MeV/amu for \DIFdelbegin \DIFdel{oxigen }\DIFdelend \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{oxygen }\DIFaddend impact. We extend \DIFdelbegin \DIFdel{it }\DIFdelend \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{our work }\DIFaddend to a universal scaling for any ion and molecule, \DIFdelbegin \DIFdel{which merges }\DIFdelend \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{merging }\DIFaddend the forty ion--molecule systems analyzed here \DIFdelbegin \DIFdel{in }\DIFdelend \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{into }\DIFaddend a single band\DIFdelbegin \DIFdel{, and }\DIFdelend \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{. Furthermore, our model }\DIFaddend proved to be valid for other molecules too.  
\end{abstract}

The good agreement shown in \DIFdelbegin \DIFdel{figure \ref{fig:zalpha} resumes }\DIFdelend \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{Fig.~\ref{fig:zalpha} summaries }\DIFaddend the main result of this work, and holds the validity of \DIFdelbegin \DIFdel{present }\DIFdelend \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{our }\DIFaddend universal scaling. Although the theoretical \DIFdelbegin \DIFdel{CDW-SSM }\DIFdelend \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{CDW--SSM }\DIFaddend results  %Present scaling rule 
are valid for energies above the maximum of the cross sections, the scaling of the experimental data extends to lower impact energies%is more extensive
, as can be noted in \DIFdelbegin \DIFdel{figure }\DIFdelend \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{Fig. }\DIFaddend \ref{fig:zalpha}.
The importance of scaling rules lay on \DIFdelbegin \DIFdel{the }\DIFdelend \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{their }\DIFaddend predictive capability. New measurements for other ions and molecules are expected to reinforce the present proposal.

\section{Conclusions}
In this letter, we present \DIFdelbegin \DIFdel{an }\DIFdelend \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{a }\DIFaddend scaling for the ionization cross sections of highly charged ions in biological targets. The scaling rule states the cross sections divided by $Z^{\alpha}$ as a function of the reduced impact energy $E/Z^{2-\alpha}$, with $\alpha=1.2$. The scaling was obtained by means of the CDW-SSM calculations for five different charged ions in eight targets and tested with the available experimental data. \DIFdelbegin \DIFdel{An }\DIFdelend \DIFaddbegin \DIFadd{A }\DIFaddend universal scaling rule  is also proposed, which reduced the cross sections with the number of active electrons of the molecule. The universal scaling proved to be valid for a large number of experimental data.
